Strategy

Mission

  • Achieve the popularization and dissemination of para sports
  • Cooperate and collaborate closely with governments, schools and related organisations around the country
  • Improve the competitiveness and the value/appeal of para-sports
  • Promote international cooperation through para-sports
  • Promote a radical transformation in public consciousness aimed at realizing an inclusive society
  • Achievement of a solid foundation for JPC

Vision

  • 2030 Vision: Achieving a vigorous, inclusive society

Focus

Philsophy of para-sports promotion:

  • Respect diversity in gender, age, nationality, values and personality, with or without disability, and aim for a society in which everyone can develop and live their individuality.
  • Sports contribute to the building of character as a member of society, as well as improving health and strength, and fostering a sense of autonomy.
  • Through sports, we can deepen society' s understanding of disability and expand the social participation of people with a disability.
Current challenges:
  • Creating an environment of inclusivity and expanding para-sports.
  • Strengthen the system for promoting a virtuous cycle that disseminates para-sports and improves competitive strength.
  • Build a loyal fanbase and promote an understanding of para-sports to further a sustainable cycle.
  • Create a solid foundation at JPC for the further development of para-sports.

Highlights of Strategy Report

To support the success of para athletes aiming for the international level:

  1. Strategic improvement of performance of top athletes.
  2. Scout and train athletes
  3. Strengthen the organizational power of JPC member organizations
Strategy to Promote Paralympic Movement:
  • Improve the value of para sports
  • Para-sports education (through ‘I’mPOSSIBLE)
  • Athlete education (improve humanity)
  • International cooperation

Operations

Sport Management


The Japanese Paralympic Commitee distributes central resources to member federal., in addition to, supporting internal competition outside of the Paralympics (Asian Para Games, Asian Youth Para Games, IBSA World Championships). The headquarters acts as a hub for training for athletes.

Staffing


The Japanese Paralympic Commitee consists of 2 chairmen, Steering Committee, affiliated communited and 64 affiliated sports organizations. Many committee members are associated with university institutions.
The Steering Committee consists of a chairman, vice chairman and 18 members which are listed below:

  • Chairperson (Director, Japan Para Sports Association)
  • Vice Chairperson (Vice President, Japan Para Sports Association)
  • Vice Chairperson (Vice President, Japan Para Sports Association)
  • Chairman of the Sports Committee of the All Japan Deaf Federation
  • Vice Chairman, Medical Committee, Japan Para Sports Association
  • Vice President, Japan Paralympians Association
  • Many other directors associated with various sports

Sustainability

Past Work

  • No information available.

Present Work

  • 5 themes of sustainability: climate change, resource management, natural environment and biodiversity, human rights, labour and fair business practices, and involvement, cooperation and communications
  • All of the electricity used at Tokyo 2020 would come from renewable sources, with a direct supply of energy being provided by Olympic and Paralympic Partner ENEOS, and through the use of tradable green power certificates
  • 24.5 tonnes of recyclable plastic waste were collected and utilized in the construction of the 98 Victory Ceremony Podiums that would be used during the Tokyo 2020 Games
  • Reduced resource consumption through efforts to simplify Games operations following the postponement, an approach that will serve as a model for future Games
  • Raised the percentage of female members of the Executive Board to 42 percent, and took a number of steps to achieve gender equality, diversity and inclusion during the Games

Is Sustainability a Value?

It is evident that the Tokyo 2020 was environmentally sustainable by the following efforts made:

  • Hydrogen-powered cauldron - produced using aluminum waste from temporary housing built after the 2011 earthquake
  • T-Shirts and trousers worn by torchbearers made from recycled plastic bottles
  • Medals made from recycled phones
  • Medalists stand on podiums made from recycled plastic waste
  • 99% of goods procured for the Games were recycled or reused
  • Athletes slept on beds made from recyclable cardboard
  • The energy came from renewable sources (solar, wood biomass power)
  • Going beyond carbon neutrality by offsetting more emissions than emitted
  • 500 hydrogen-electric vehicles provided by Toyota
  • Only eight venues were built from scratch.

Social sustainability efforts at Tokyo 2020 include:
  • Gender balance in terms of athletes
  • First official Pride House aimed at raising awareness of LGBTQ issues
  • The Department of Female Athlete Medicine in the village has been set up for the first time for medical support for female athletes
  • 12 additional woman were appointed to the Executive Board (boosting female representation from 20 to 40%)
  • The Organizing Committee has a department dedicated to diversity and inclusion
