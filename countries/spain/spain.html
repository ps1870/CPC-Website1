Operations

Sport Management


SPC provides services and funding for athletes and sports federations. Spanish sports federations are responsible for the preparation (training, support, competitions) of athletes for their respective sports. Each sport has a monitoring coordinator that develops monthly reports that outline the progress of each athlete and overall assist with training.

Services available to athletes from SPC include:

  • Nutrition (dietitians, doctors, and psychologists)
  • Psychology service
  • Coach professional development program
  • Physiotherapy/Physiology - Paralympic specialists
  • Medical - specific medical centers available for Paralympic athletes
  • Athlete care service (scholarships, equipment support, training and education)

Staffing

SPC consists of a general assembly, executive committee, guarantee commission and standing commission. The general assembly is made up of representatives from sports federations, associations related to paralympic sport and leaders.

Social Change DE&I

ADOP Plan

  • Aims to provide Spanish Paralympic athletes with the best conditions to be able to carry out their preparation for the paralympic games with the financial contribution from several large Spanish companies
  • Scholarship program: financial aid so athletes can focus on their sports
  • Services program: comprehensive training support system

Women and Sport

  • Committed to making it possible for girls and women to participate, compete and develop their careers in the field of sport
  • Pregnancy and Maternity benefit: scholarships are provided from the moment the baby is reported until 8-months after the birth of the baby

Iberdola Spain Foundation Scholarships

  • Scholarships for university and postgraduate training are available for athletes who receive funding from the ADOP plan

Sustainability

Past Work

  • No information available

Present Work

  • Paralympic Bus 25 Visits The Headquarters Of The Ministry Of Social Rights to commemorate the 25th anniversary of CPE with the reforestation of 25,000 trees in the Madrid municipal area
  • In June 2021, Paralympic uniforms were designed using sustainable materials.

Spanish Olympic Committee Sustainability Efforts

March 2022

  • The Spanish NOC included Sustainable Development Goals 1 and 2 in its sustainable development strategy, which is focused on eliminating poverty and hunger in society
November 2021
  • Spanish NOC congress discusses sustainable development in the Olympic Movement, sustainability programmes with partners (energy, gender equality, education and sustainable mobility)
February 2021
  • Ministry of Ecological Transition and Demographic Challenges (MITECO), the Ministry of Culture and Sport and the Spanish NOC have signed an agreement to promote collaboration on environmental and social sustainability and the dissemination of the values of Olympism
2018
  • Developed a sustainability strategy aligned with the IOC Sustainability Strategy
    • Infrastructure: three-year agreement with Ibereólica, which will improve energy efficiency and develop renewable energies at the COE's headquarters.
    • Mobility: Sponsorship agreement with Toyota Spain, which will provide hybrid vehicles until 2020 Agreements with other companies in the transport sector are currently being negotiated.
    • Internal operations: A sponsorship agreement for cloud services and digitalization is currently being negotiated.
    • Gender equality: Agreement to support a training and coaching programme to help women take leadership roles in the sports sector is currently being negotiated.
    • Waste: The COE has decided to eliminate plastic bottles by replacing them with glass bottles carrying a slogan that reflects the Olympic values.


Is Sustainability a Value?

CPE’s sustainability efforts appear to be minimal with the focus being on reforestation efforts, but more prevalent efforts made by their Olympic Team. COE’s strategy aims to apply sustainable policies in internal and external operations to reduce carbon footprint and strives to be an inspiration to the Olympic movement in Spain and to Spanish society. At the COE Sustainability Summit, COE and its partners will present solutions to reduce and offset the carbon footprint of sport with three innovative initiatives: COE’s Seal of Sustainable Sport Organisation, COE’s Seal of Sustainable Sport Event, as well as the Spanish Olympic Forest to offset the carbon footprint.
