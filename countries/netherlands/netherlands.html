Strategy

Mission

  • Create optimal sports conditions for everyone: from recreational user to top athlete, from disabled athlete to volunteer and fan
  • Team NL’s mission is to inspire and connect the Netherlands from the nation’s sport performance

Vision

  • Sport and physical activity contribute to the vitality, health and social development of every individual and thus to a happy, vital and inclusive Netherlands
  • Believe in the power of sport

Focus

  • Everyone no matter their skill level will enjoy daily sports, which will be achieved by providing a socially safe, health and sustainable environment

Highlights of Strategy Report

Core activities:

  1. Contribute to the development of the quality of the Dutch sports sector
  2. Promote sports participation and top sports performance
  3. Generating and distributing income for various (sports) programs
  4. Sending top teams to Olympic and Paralympic games
  5. Representing Dutch sport in international organizations
  6. Promote the fundamental principles and values of Olympianism and monitor compliance with the Olympic Charter
  7. Promote the significance of sport in society

Operations

Sport Management


NOC*NSF consists of the Olympic and Paralympic Committee for the Netherlands. The organization provides centralized resources (education, training facilities, development pathways) for athletes and coaches in conjunction with the 77 national sports federations and 16 associations. There are two different streams/departments that manage sport, sports participation beginning from the grassroots sports development and top sport focusing on high-performance elite sport. NOC*NSF has four pillars to lead how they manage sports which are, responsibility, democracy, society and transparency.

Resources provided by the Top Sports department of NOC*NSF include:

  • 5 High-performance training centers available for national-level athletes - all facilities in one place (utilized by 1000 athletes from 30 sports) - combined para and able-bodied
  • Full-time, elite senior athletes are eligible for top sport status - access to funding and resources
  • A number of different resources and models for long-term athlete and coach development
  • TeamNL talent days to identify up-and-coming talent

Staffing

NOC*NSF consists of a board, a board of directors, and a general meeting. The board consists of the chairman, general manager (Marc van den Tweel) and the business manager (John Bierling ). The two overarching departments are Top Sport and Sport Participation which are supported by the following departments, corporate affairs, partnerships & commerce and communication & media, while ICT, HR, Legal and finance assist with the overall organization.

External

Relationship with NOC

The Athens 2024 Paralympic Games was the first time that the NOC*NSF was responsible for the preparation of Dutch Paralympic athletes. By 2007, NOC*NSF becomes an official member of the International Paralympic Committee.

Mission: create ideal circumstances for everyone who aspires to take part in sport and physical activities; from recreational sportsmen and sportswomen to elite athletes and from people with an impairment to volunteers and fans.

Social Change DE&I

Sport for Everyone

  • NOC*NSF wants everyone to be able to participate in sports and feel a sense of belonging especially low-income individuals and members of the LGBTQ+ community

Sustainability and Sport

  • NOC*NSF wants to stimulate the development, realization and maintenance of sustainable sports accommodations
  • 3 Ps for a better future for people and the environment: people, planet and profit
  • Provide a tool for owners of sports facilities to help make their accommodations more sustainable

Safe Sport

  • To combat inappropriate conduct like doping, match-fixing, sexual intimidation, violation of human rights and fraud
  • People can report behaviour such as those mentioned above to the Center for Safe Sports

Sustainability

Past Work

  • No information available.

Present Work

  • Sport NL Green is an online tool that provides free advice from energy coaches on how to make their sports accommodations more sustainable.
    • Owners of sports facilities can map possibilities on how to be more sustainable, such as providing information on national and regional subsidies and financing options
  • Coaching Programs Sports Clubs
    • Green Club - sport-wide initiative to make sports facilities more sustainable
    • Sports room - supporting sports associations that want to become energy neutral
    • Sports power 12 - guides sports associations in making them more sustainable
    • Sporty cheerful - supporting sports providers who want to become more sustainable
    • ENGIE Sports Match - ENGIE helps sports facilities to become more sustainable through advice and guidance throughout the country

Is Sustainability a Value?

NOC*NSF wants to stimulate the development, realization and preservation of sustainable sports accommodations, as well as leverage hydrogen as a sustainable energy source. NOC*NSF plans to achieve its goal by developing a sustainability coaching program, an incentive contribution for Promising Innovation Projects for the purpose of making associations more sustainable and loans aimed at sustainable construction.

Their partnership with ‘Mission H2’ aims to put hydrogen on the map as a sustainable alternative energy source, and introduce the rest of the country to this sustainable energy carrier with the help of the Olympic power of sport.

The target for 2032 is that Athletes will rate their sports environment with an 8 or higher based on availability, accessibility, affordability, sustainability and quality.
