Strategy

Mission

  • Paralympics Australia (PA) aims to be the leading National Paralympic Committee and one of Australia’s leading sports administration organizations.

Core Values

  • World class sports performance through excellence in the delivery of our business and the development of our people.

Focus

  • The delivery and performance of the Australian Paralympic Team is the primary focus and a key element of each Paralympics Australia business unit.
  • Have a constitutional mandate to coordinate, represent and promote the Paralympic movement in Australia.
  • Sporting excellence, in all its dimensions, is an overarching goal of Paralympics Australia and forms a key measure for ongoing evaluation.

Highlights of 2019 Strategy Plan

  1. PA has a multi-year corporate and budget strategy with priority investing in para-sport performance, expanding its commercial portfolio, deepening stakeholder engagement and growing its paralympic brand.
  2. Optimizing Australian high-performance system outcomes through a prioritized and individualized approach based on sport performance needs and impact.
  3. Drive sustainable, repeatable success at a level and spread of sports and campaigns that is the best possible for Australia’s talent pool and affordability.
  4. Deliver a balanced approach that resonates with all Australians, consistently generating pride, inspiration and long-lasting community support.

Summer Games

  • Total summer medal count: 1205
  • Top performing summer sports of all time (by medal count): athletics, swimming, cycling
  • Typical number of athletes (in recent Games): 170+ in recent Games

Winter Games

  • Top performing winter sports of all time (by medal count): alpine skiing and snowboarding, which was their only representation in recent Games
  • Typical number of athletes (in recent Games): 9-12 athletes

Development Highlights of Top Performing Sports

The Paralympic Pathway System was introduced in 2011 to transition athletes from state to national level of competition by addressing three areas: education, environment and experience.

Swimming: Athletes progress through state and territory development program, SA Para Performance program and then Australian Dolphins swim team selection.

Athletics: Target Talent Program and National Athelte Support Structure (NASS)

Sport Management


Paralympics Australia is comprised of 23 national sport federations and national sporting organizations all of which are voting members within the organization. The key stakeholders include the Australian government (sport Australia), Australian Institute of Sport and IPC.

National sport federations oversee the development of sports from grassroots to elite with support in the form of funding and resources from Paralympics Australia, Australian Institute of Sport and Sport Australia. Paralympic Australia partnered with Australian Institute of Sport, National Sport Organizations and Commonwealth Games Committee on several intiatives leading up to Tokyo 2020, Beijing 2022 and upcoming Commonwealth Games for innovative equipment, safety policies for athletes, education in sports science, strength and conditioning and COVID-19 policies. Participation and pathway coordinators attempt to educate and spread Paralympic sport around the country, in addition to encouraging individuals to get involved at the grassroots level (beginnning of the high-performance pathway).

Athlete Representation

Athlete Commission

  • Established in 2017 with the intention of facilitating meaningful engagement with athletes
  • Made up of 11 Paralympians across 8 sports

“Athlete Connection” Highlights

  • True North Research’s 2021 Benchmark report found the Australian Paralympic Team to have the highest score on the emotional connection of any Australian sports team following Tokyo 2020
  • Ranked first in dimensions of respect, trust and pride and second in enjoyment and bond

Policies and Frameworks

  • Encompasses policies related to athlete protection and safety with a focus on safe sport, fair sport and inclusive sport
  • Key policies include: The People Protection Policy, Codes of Conduct, Anti-Match Fixing, and Anti-Doping

Athlete Programs

Community programs designed to support Paralympic athletes include:

  • The Paralympic Speakers Program
  • Indigenous PWD & Sport
  • Pride in Sport
  • Welcoming Australia (social support for young people with disabilities)
  • Paralympic Workplace Diversity Program (connect Paralympians) with employment opportunities
  • History Project (preserve and share Paralympic movement in Australia)

External

Relationshiop with NOC

There is no direct connection between Australia’s Olympic and Paralympic committees. According to the Australian Paralympic Committee website, Australian Olympic Committee is listed though as a partner. Both committees share a partnership through PacificAusSports, in which the Australian Government, AOC and APC supported athletes from various Pacific regions leading up to Tokyo 2020. This was created in response to the challenges that COVID-19 created for Pacific regions to compete in Olympic qualifiers.

Social Change DE&I

Para-sport Equipment Fund

  • A partnership between Paralympic Australia, Sport Australia, and Paralympic Australia’s sponsors and donors to alleviate the costs of Para-sport equipment for athletes, especially entry-level athletes

Indigenous PWD & Sport

  • A webpage dedicated to providing information and resources to help with chronic disease prevention and promote health and physical activity awareness and opportunities for aboriginal groups

Welcoming Australia

  • Uses sports engagement and other support programs to work with children and young people from migrant, refugee and asylum seeker backgrounds that also live with a disability
  • Help young people with disabilities develop stronger ties to the community (and sport) with increased social participation

Pride in Sport

  • Paralympics Australia partners with Australian Pride in Sport program to further develop and enhance LGBTQ inclusion

Paralympic Workplace Diversity Program (PWDP)

  • Support current and retired Paralympic athletes in career development
  • Appointed ‘360 HR Consulting’ based in Sydney to manage the PWDP
  • Employers register to become Paralympic Employment Providers
  • The APC website has a list of organizations that are Paralympic Employment Providers and the athletes that work there

Sustainability

Past Work

  • Campaign to sustain participation in sport by potential Paralympic athletes through the Green and Gold Virtual Seat Campaign
    • Funds raised through the campaign help Para-athletes compete and provide those with disabilities the opportunity to participate in sport. Ultimately recognizes and develops future Olympians.

Present Work

  • Collaborating with the Sports Environment Alliance (SEA) which seeks to promote sustainability by educating staff on sustainability issues and best practices.
  • SMART goals on sustainability focus on:
    • New regional enterprise, living and employment precincts
    • Australia’s first net zero carbon region
    • Australia’s first circular economy region

Is Sustainability a Value?

Sustainability is stated to be a value for PA whose six pillars of focus are on goals they and their partners will pursue including, “aligning with sustainable development goals.” The CEO of PA has the vision to empower Para-sport excellence and advocate for inclusion with a key driver including sustainability. The Australian Sports Commission states that Australian sport needs to be carried forward through generations, building sustainable improvement and success.

Para-Sport Equipment Fundensures the sustainability of newer programs — it is an initiative between PA and Sport Australia that aims to address a high barrier to sport for those with disabilities (high costs and difficult access to equipment).
