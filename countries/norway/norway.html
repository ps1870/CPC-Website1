Strategy

Mission

Sports enjoyment for everyone

Core Values

NIF shall work to ensure that all people are given the opportunity to practice sports based on their wishes and needs, and without being subjected to unreasonable and disproportionate discrimination.

Focus

Inclusivity for people with disabilities to be included in sports and it demands targeted and reinforced measures. This goal will be achieved by NIF through:

  • broad sports
  • top sports
  • Competence
  • Communication
  • broad sports
  • Facilities
  • Activity aids, sign language interpreter and companion arrangements
  • Partnerships and collaborators

Highlights of Strategy Report

‘The Sport Will!’ is NIF’s long term plan from 2019-2023.

  • Improve society by contributing to a more physically active society and sustainable development.
  • Create a better framework conditions for sports in Norway, in the form of strengthening volunteering, improving the economy and strengthening the competence in the entire organization.

Operations

Sport Management and Staffing


NIF has a district approach to sports management in which different councils represent sports development for their designated region, and NIF provides organizational and financial support. Large decisions are made in collaboration with NIF and the councils. There are 55 special associations, 11 sports clubs and 328 sports councils. NIF has a biennial Sports Council meeting and a Sports Council to unify sports councils.

External

Relationship with NOC

Norwegian Sports Confederation oversees all sports in the country, including the Olympic and Paralympic committees. Olympic and Paralympic athletes compete under one Team Norway The Norwegian Sports Confederation ensure all people are given the opportunity to practice sports based on their wishes and needs, and without being subjected to unreasonable and disproportionate discrimination.

Social Change DE&I

Plastic-Smart Sports Initiative

  • Norwegian Sports Confederation collaborates with WWF World Wide in 2019 to identify plastic use in sports and find ways to reduce it

Bullying

  • Coaches and leaders are informed on how to deal with a situation of bullying

Inclusion

  • To ensure children of immigrant and low-income families can participate in sports
  • Resources can be found on why and how to include those individuals including a free online course
  • NIF also offers a subsidy for children who face income or cultural barriers to sport

Sexual Harassment and Abuse

  • Steps are provided in the event someone involved in sport experiences abuse or witnesses it including educational videos on their website
  • Every person who is a member of the Norwegian Sports Confederation and who works in a position of power or trust toward children must present a police certificate

Violence and Threats

  • Zero tolerance of any form of discrimination and harassment
  • Principles when dealing with cases of violence and threats are outlined on their website including how to contact the police

Sustainability

Past Work

  • Partnered with Youth Education through Sport (YES) to build a community-based sports initiative targeting children and young people as a platform to raise awareness on key issues impacting their lives including HIV/AIDS.

Present Work

  • NIF works actively throughout Europe and at the global level to influence international sport
  • Partners in southern Africa to promote values of good governance, sustainability and fair play

Is Sustainability a Value?

NIF emphasizes the importance of sustainability on their website and prioritizes 5 sustainability goals (good health and quality of life, gender equality, less inequality, responsible consumption and production, stop climate change). Its website highlights sports sustainability efforts with projects such as Plastic-Smart sports, a collaboration with the WWF World Wide Fund to map plastic use and strategize ways to reach out with relevant measures for sports. Youth Olympics in Lillehammer became the first sporting event in Norway to comply with ISO 20121, an international standard for sustainable events.
