Strategy

Mission

  • The coordination and representation of all the sports federations (41 members) in order to develop the sports practice of people with disabilities.
  • The constitution and direction of the French team at the Paralympic Games.

Core Values

  • The coordination and representation of sports federations that offer sports for people with disabilities.

Focus

  • The influence of paralympic sport, in particular through the performance of the teams of France.
  • Development of para-sports in France.
  • Strengthening the Paralympic identity within French sport.

Highlights of Strategy Report

  • From 2018-2020, the CPSF's projected budget is 7.7 million euros.
  • Three main projects:
    1. Targeting sports where the French has limited or no representation and creating an environment for athletes in these sports to succeed.
    2. Increasing the visibility of para-sports by rebranding CPSF.
    3. Creating a Paralympic referent in all French regions and potentially overseas representation as well. CPSF is currently approaching potential partners, prioritizing internal communication aimed at its members and defining the role of referents.

Development Highlights of Top Performing Sports

La Relève System was introduced in 2019 in reponse to France's failure to appear in the top 10 for summer Games since 2004. This program identifies indivduals aged 16-35 and help them with their athletic development and involvement in para-sport for a day in hopes to find podium talent for the 2024 Games.


Operations

Sport Management and Staffing


There are 41 member federations that fall into one of these three categories: College of Founding Federations, College of Paralympic Federations and College of Associated Sports.

The College of Founding Federations is made up of two organizations, the French Handisport Federation (focus on motor and sensory disabilities) and the French Federation of Adapted Sport (focus on mental and psychological disabilities), that oversee many sports depending on the kind of disability and act as a governing body for a number of para-sports. Both of these organizations play a significant role in the development of various para sports and resources (coaching, leagues, practitioners, clubs and licenses) across the country.

Staffing

CPSF has 27 staff members and the board consists of 12 members that oversee the organization. Each region of France has a Paralympic representative to work with local organization to develop para-sports.

Leadership consists of:

  • President
  • General director
  • Sports director
  • Communication director
  • Head of Paralympic division
  • Development projects and research lead

Athlete Representation


No assigned athlete representation position or committee on the CPSF board

Athlete Ambassadors

  • There are 18 athlete ambassadors (alumni and current athletes) for the French Paralympic and Sports Committee
  • Their role is to raise awareness for Paralympic sport by sharing their experience and spreading the committee’s values and history of the Paralympic movement

External

Relationship with NOC

Goal

French National and Olympic Sports Committee (CNOSF) and the French Paralympic and Sports Committee currently sit together on the organizing committee for Paris 2024.

Olympic and Paralympic Week was created as a part of the Paris 2024 organization plan, with a mission of building awareness of Olympic and Paralympic values, discovering Olympic and Paralympic disciplines, and educating young people on disabilities and encouraging their participation in sports. Young people were engaged by using sports in the education of class subjects such as maths, history, geography and English, and sports and exercise.

In May 2022, France National Sports Agency launched “Coaches 2024” which aimed to support success at Olympic and Paralympic games. This was designed to enhance the coachs’ skills of both the Olympic and Paralympic teams ahead of Paris 2024 by recruiting expert coaches.

Social Change DE&I

WeThe15 Campaign

  • Created by the French Paralympic committee and part of the Paris 2024 organization plan to fight against the discrimination against people with disabilities
  • 15 comes from 1.2 billion people worldwide living with physical or mental disabilities (15% of the population)
  • Creating programs to empower greater inclusion for people with disabilities within society

Gender Equality Agreement

  • Ahead of Paris 2024, an agreement was signed to promote gender equality
  • The president of the French Paralympic committee could not attend the meeting but is expected to commit to gender equality
  • There are 6 themes in the agreement:
    1. Promote female leadership and equality of women
    2. Enhance gender balance in governance
    3. Respond to gender-based violence
    4. Closing the investment gap and promoting equal economic opportunity
    5. Support equal participation
    6. Ensure equal opportunities for girls in fields of sport and physical activity

Sustainability

Past Work

  • Signature of a memorandum of understanding between the IOC and the IPC that long term (2032) their goal is to increase the visibility of the Paralympic Games, strengthen the existing cooperation, ensure the financial stability/sustainability and the viability of the CPI

Present Work

Sustainability is a key focus of Paris 2024
  • Relying on 95% of existing/temporary sites to reduce the carbon impact
  • The carbon impact of all activities to the Games will be measured and monitored
  • Carbon emissions that cannot be avoided, Paris has projects to spread it over 5 continents to restore and conserve forests and oceans, and avoidance projects such as supplying improved stoves to populations dependent on rudimentary cooking tools
  • For the 2024 Games, Paris innovates solutions for 100% renewable energy during the games, circular economy, sustainable food, responsible digital technology, etc.
  • Paris 2024 Environmental Commitments has action plans for all environmental priorities, a carbon method and system, governance and responsibility and a sustainable management system for the organizing committee

Is Sustainability a Value?

Sustainability is a growing issue globally and France’s plans to host the Games and indicative that they are taking steps to implement more sustainable practices as the host country.
