Strategy

Mission

BPA Mission: To make the UK world leading in Paralympic Sport on and off the field.
Strategic Plan Mission: To enable world-leading performance at the Paralympic Games and to drive positive, sustainable change in UK society

Core Values

Strategic Plan Vision: Through sport, inspire a better world for disabled people.

Focus

BPA's mission will be achieved through the excellence of the team at the Games and by creating advocacy for the movement. BPA Article’s that fit this focus:

  • To relieve people with physical, sensory and learning disabilities by encouraging and facilitating their participation in sporting activities and assisting in the provision of training, facilities and equipment for this purpose.
  • The relief of poverty and the advancement of education and such other purposes for the benefit of the community as shall be exclusively charitable.

Highlights of Strategy Report

Be the best prepared team, which will be achieved by:

  • Commitment is to provide a positive team experience from start to finish
  • Mitigate risk by being more proactive
  • Focus areas
    • Create & maintain a positive team culture and strong team identity
    • Unrivaled knowledge of team and environments
    • Unparalleled Games readiness
    • Create & maintain optimal team environments
Social impact
  • To see a more inclusive environment for disabled people in the UK, in sport and in society
  • Focus areas
    • A shift in public attitudes to disability, where people recognise the capabilities and capacities of disabled people as well as the barriers they face
    • An improvement in the lived experience of disabled people
    • An environment that enables disabled people to thrive
Challenges
  1. Ensuring the BPA’s financial health and sustainability throughout the pandemic and beyond.
  2. Ensuring they are able to send well-prepared teams to Paralympic Games in 3 years, with athlete welfare being a top priority.
  3. Ensuring the charity is able to create a strong impact on the lives of disabled people in the UK by a combination of partner-funded activities and ability to inspire by telling the stories of our Paralympic athletes.

Operations

Sport Management


BPA consists of several members who oversee each sport, also any other party that has an impact on Paralympic Sport. BPA is registered as a charity and reaps the benefits that it entails. NGBs oversee their respective sport and are responsible for the development of the sport from grassroots to elite as well as overseeing Olympic and Paralympic teams (most oversee both para and non-para sports). There are voting and non-voting members at general meetings.

Voting members are:

  1. Each NGB - one for each Paralympic sport
  2. Disability sports organizations -one for each disability
  3. One body responsible for the development of disabled sport in each country (one for each country - England, Northern Ireland, Scotland, Wales)
Non-voting members are:
  1. the Board
  2. Individual members (honorary vice presidents)
  3. Sports organizations that offer a sport are no longer in the Paralympics
  4. 3 athlete representatives including the chair of the athlete’s commission (these organizations can attend meetings of the association and the NPC but cannot vote at them)

Staffing

Several different committees give advice to the Board (reporting structure below).

Athlete Representation

Athletes’ Commission for the British Paralympic Association

  • Representing athletes’ perspective to BPA Board, Executive Team and Games Planning Group
  • The mission statement includes ensuring the BPA takes an athlete-centred approach
  • Made up by elected athlete representatives

British Athlete’s Commission

  • Duty to care for and promote the welfare of all members especially children, young people and adults at risk
  • Staff members are trained to uphold safeguarding led by Lead Safeguarding Officer and Deputy Safeguarding Officer
  • Policies and procedures include: privacy, safeguarding children and adults, accessibility, anti-corruption, equality and diversity, investigation of public interest disclosure and conflicts of interest

BPA Safeguarding

  • Athlete voice and representation are at the forefront of the organization
  • Works in collaboration with BPA and other organizations (NGOs, UK Sport and partners) in the high-performance sport system
  • Offers athletes confidential, independent support and services surrounding a range of issues pertaining to athlete safety and well-being
  • Athlete support is provided in several areas specifically, mental health, disciplinary and grievances, classification, mediation, selection and anti-doping
  • Membership is given to athletes funded by the World Class Programme or through NGOs for athletes that are not in WCP but competing at an international level to become members of the BAC

External

Relationship with NOC

Great Britain’s Olympic and Paralympic committees are separate entities, where Olympic and Paralympic athletes are either represented by Team GB (Olympics) or Paralympics GB (Paralympics). Both committees are funded through the same fund from the UK Government.

Social Change DE&I

Equality & Diversity

  • British Paralympics Association complies with the Code for Sports Governance of having a minimum of 30% of each gender on the Board with the aim of achieving greater gender parity

BPA Equality and Diversity Policy

  • Detailed policy located on their site that seeks to ensure equality for those who are unlawfully discriminated against because of age, gender reassignment, religion or beliefs, sex, sexual orientation, race, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, or disability.

BPA Diversity Action Plan

  • Three areas of focus: recruitment, engagement, and progressing talent from within
  • Recruitment: attract a diverse range of candidates
  • Engagement: commitment to diversity is communicated through internal practices and externally
  • Progressing talent from within: develop a strong internal pipeline of diverse talent to populate decision-making and other structures

BPA Equality and Diversity Data

  • Breakdown of staff into various categories that they identify as or belong to (ethnicity, gender, pregnancy, age, disability, sexual orientation, marital status, religion)

Sustainability

Past Work

  • EDF (Electricity supplier of London 2012) helped the (BPA) achieve global accreditation for sustainability during the London 2012 Games
    • Assessed waste management, energy efficiency, water consumption, transport, sustainability, education, and health promotion
    • Worked with waste management specialists to improve the environmental impact of athletes
    • Implemented low carbon transport to ensure local, organic and fair-trade food was provided to athletes

Present Work

  • Channel 4 coverage aimed to build on innovation within its broadcasting at the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games
  • Athlete uniforms made with sustainable innovations such as PRIME GREEN (high-performance recycled material that uses minimum of 40% recycled content) and PRIMEBLUE (high-performance recycled yarn containing at least 50% Parley Ocean Plastic

Is Sustainability a Value?

While sustainability is mentioned in BPA’s ten-year strategy plan, no in-depth metrics are provided nor are their future sustainability efforts clear. BPA’s 10-year strategic plan mentions the importance of reducing environmental impact and states with partners they will “build and deliver a credible approach to environmental sustainability and measurable reductions in our carbon footprint over the next ten years.” In addition, financial sustainability is a long-term strategic objective with operating plans built around it through budget setting, review and auditing processes.
