Focus

The Düsseldorf Declaration is the result of the Association Culture Conference that aims to improve culture and cooperations with DBS. This states:

  • Represent sports for people with disabilities in Germany.
  • Use self-image adopted on the Association Day in Leipzig in 2017 as the basis of for joint actions.
  • Build strong state, professional and federal associations in order implement a self-image.
  • Overall message of doing business with tolerance, honesty, openness and mutual respect.

Highlights from Association Day 2017 in Leipzig

Common overarching strategic association goals for the federal, state and professional associations. These goals are focused on:

  1. Encourage sports for people with disabilities.
  2. Social recognition of sport for people with disabilities.
  3. High-quality rehabilitation sports options close to home for people with disabilities, those with impending disabilities and chronic illnesses.
  4. Basis for a wide range of high-quality sports.
  5. Strengthened international competitiveness of top-level Paralympic sport.

Development Highlights of Top Performing Sports


Paralympic Squad is a support program for medal candidate athletes to help them prepare for the upcoming Games.

Perspective Squad is a support program for future medal candidates to compete at top levels with the goal of Games participation.

Youth Elite Program is a support program for young talent (including World Championship and European Championship winners and Paralympians) and supports their development into top-performing Paralympic athletes.


Operations

Sport Management


DBS is responsible for sports for disabled people throughout the country, including the national Paralympic team. DBSJ (German Disabled Youth Sports) is an independent organization within DBS that promotes participation and development of sports for youth with disabilities.

The federal Paralympic training centers are designated by DBS that are equipped for high-performance sports development. Each sport has its own association within the organization that oversees the sport in terms of training, development and high performance. In addition, each sport is categorized as Paralympic sports, Non-Paralympic sports and national games.

  • 17 State associations
  • 2 Professional associations - German Wheelchair Sports Association and German Sports Association for the Hard of Hearing
  • 7 Extraordinary members
  • 6300 clubs with 600,000 members


An annual conference with associate members in which each delegate represents one of the following organizations with one vote:

  • German Board of Trustees for Therapeutic Riding (DKThR)
  • Social Association Germany (SoVD)
  • German Blind and Visually Impaired Association (DBSV)
  • Special Olympic Germany (SOD)
  • German Rowing Association (DRV)
  • Disabled Golf Club Germany
  • German-Turkish Association for the Promotion of Disabled Sports
  • TransDia Sport Germany Registered Association

External

Relationship with NOC

Germany’s Paralympic and Olympic teams have collaborated and for the first time competed together under Team Germany at the Tokyo 2020 Games. Furthermore, they share the same sports marketing partner Deutsche Sport Marketing.

Social Change DE&I

Equality and Diversity

  • The German Disabled Sports Youth organization is an independent organization within the German Paralympic Committee
  • It works to provide young people with the opportunity to take part in sports
  • The largest German youth population participating in sports with a disability: 7-14 years old (28.85%)

Paralympic Youth Camp

  • ‘German Disabled Sports Youth’ invites youth to international Paralympic Youth Camps at all Paralympic summer and winter games, including youth without disabilities
  • Purpose: allow young people to witness the Games, explore the host country and learn about different cultures

Index for Inclusion in and through Sport

  • Developed by the German Paralympic Committee with the goals of developing and expanding an inclusive sports landscape, raising awareness of inclusion in and through sport and guiding sports associations/clubs on implementation
  • The index offers aid to reviewing one’s own organizational situation and making changes if need be
  • Index flyers are available on their website for public use

Sustainability

Past Work

  • DBS Vice President emphasized the importance of committing to disable sports and an integrative society after the London 2012 Games

Present Work

  • Three weeks of educational workshops before Tokyo 2020 at a Paralympic youth camp to educate participants on their ecological footprints and how to be sustainable

Is Sustainability a Value?

The President of DBS urges the IOC to choose more sustainable Games hosts. Emphasis on the importance of sustainability, climate and environmental protection from the global effects of the Games.
